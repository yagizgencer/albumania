Direction 1 — Podium & Pool

A ranked Top 5 podium sits on top; below it, all tracks live in a pool. Each pool row has a single ★ button that drops the track into the next open podium slot (one button per row, not five). Reorder or remove inside the podium.

Feels: clear goal-oriented flow, very tidy. Trade-off: two zones = more vertical space.

    Direction 2 — Tap to Rank

    One single list, no separate zones. Tap a track and it joins your Top 5 with a number badge — in tap order. Tap again to drop it (the rest renumber). A slim medal strip up top shows the current podium.

    Feels: the simplest possible — one gesture, zero buttons, very cozy. Trade-off: reordering means re-tapping; best when order-of-choice is good enough.

      Direction 3 — Rank on Demand

      Keeps the current model of assigning exact positions, but hides the clutter: each row has one small “Rank ▾” chip that opens a tiny 1–5 popover only when you click it. Taken positions are disabled. Set positions show as a teal “#2” chip.

      Feels: closest to today, full control over exact slots, professional. Trade-off: one extra click per pick vs. a straight tap.

        Direction 4 — Drag Podium

        A playful medal podium (gold / silver / bronze / 4 / 5) as a sticky sidebar. Drag tracks from the pool onto a medal — no per-row buttons at all. (Tip: in this mock you can also click a pool row to fill the next open medal.)

        Feels: the most characterful & tactile, great on desktop. Trade-off: drag needs a touch-friendly fallback for mobile.
